Unit 2: Reflection

As always, it takes until Unit 2 or Unit 3 to really get going with a new subject. Generally, I really enjoy the way these modules are structured - especially the 12-Unit modules. When studying these modules, you have enough time to get used to the topic before the first assignment comes up. Additionally, I'm glad that this module is taught before the dissertation and capstone project, because it explains both the literature review and the research proposal, which will makee it easier for us to choose topics and come up with a plan for our masters thesis. I have continued to think about topics for my dissertation. In addition to the topics I mentioned in last weeks' topics, I've added the following:

  • Developing Machine Learning Tools for Identifying and Disrupting Illegal Marketplaces on the Dark Web
  • Smart Routers which are immune to deauthentication attacks
  • Automated OSINT Framework for Cybersecurity Threat Detection
  • Smart Contract Vulnerability Scanner using AI
  • How AI Models could aid/replace Tower Controllers

I'm yet to find something that I really want to pursue. I am struggling a little bit with finding a topic I can research and write about, but also produce some sort of artefact for. I'll stick to planning and researching topics for now, hopefully I'll find something worth pursuing in the coming days.
